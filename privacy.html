b. Proximity services technology

One of the technologies that Cheekd uses is called “Proximity” Services. The platforms and applications that use this technology are called “Proximity Platform.” Proximity Platform works by collecting the “information” that smart phones and other wireless devices transmit publicly for the very purpose of being discovered and making electronic connections with other devices – sort of like nametags at a conference.

Cheekd is using proximity services that have created an evolution of location-based services. It is also important for you to know that we do not and cannot collect any “content” from any of your calls, emails, posts, tweets, etc., or other communications that go over your smartphone. We just collect the device addresses, and then it is up to you to link whatever other information or services that you choose to associate with a tagged device. This association feature can incorporate many types of your favorite social media, including your Twitter user name, Facebook name or location data like a Foursquare venue and more—as long as you had previously chosen to make those profiles or feeds unrestricted and public in your privacy settings.

For our service to work, you need to enable your phone hardware settings for Bluetooth, WiFi, Near Field Communications (NFC if you have it) and GPS and in some instances you need to have a cellular or WiFi data connection to the Internet. Cheekd Proximity Platform and other similar applications and its technology license partners work because smartphones and other wireless devices have radio transmitters that let them connect to networks or other devices through the air. Each of these radios has a number or name that is unique to that piece of equipment here we call it “Equipment Identifiers ”. This “Equipment Identifiers” that is broadcast into the air so that other devices can “discover” and connect with them.

Our technologies let your smartphone discover these radio addresses and “tag” them so you can tell when you and your smartphone get close to other Cheekd user device. Cheekd collects and uses these unique equipment identifiers so that users like you know when they are the same physical space with other devices whose radio addresses they have tagged. We call this a “proximity” application because users can get notified when a device they’ve tagged comes into or goes out of range, or make certain things happen automatically when you and your smartphone get close to another device you’ve tagged.

“Proximity” for our purposes means how close are you to the device you’ve tagged:

  • Range for Bluetooth transmitters is around 30 feet.
  • Range for NFC transmitters is under an inch.
  • Range for GPS is around 30 feet.

While smartphones and other gadgets publicly broadcast a number of different unique “Equipment Identifiers,” Cheekd only collects and uses the publicly discoverable names and numbers for WiFi, Bluetooth, GPS, Near-Field-Communications (NFC) devices and some cellular tower location data depending on the technology or carrier. When you sign up to use our service, Cheekd allows you to discover and tag your devices by their unique WiFi, Bluetooth, GPS or NFC addresses. These are based on users’ devices that broadcast publicly.
